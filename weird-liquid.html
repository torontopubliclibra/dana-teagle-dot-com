weird liquid

an archive of short-form writings of varying style and quality by dana "rusty" rosamund teagle (a.k.a. the toronto public libra).

if I think about where I felt the most peace as a young person, my mind races to three specific places, all nested together in the downtown strip of the city where I grew up: the public library, the used book store, and the independent comic book shop. when I felt most alone and strange, when it felt like all my problems were unprecedented and no one understood me, there was always books. I think I found the woman I am today in those dusty volumes and plastic-wrapped single issues.

written by dana rusty on 14/01/2024

I've said this before but I believe it bears repeating that many who speak passionately on issues online often have clearly failed to internalize that the discourse, causes, identities, and dynamics that are feeling ever so personal and specific and of the moment to them have existed and been long tired even prior to their learning about it 2-3 years ago. the more time you spend deep in libraries and archives and histories, whether paper or digital, the clearer the cyclical nature of it all becomes, and even more apparent than that is the need to defend and advocate for our legal and material rights above all else, including but not limited to inter-community squabbles.

written by dana rusty on 09/01/2024

when we reduce where a trans person is at in their transition to "x # of years" we fail to take into account the ways that class, race, income, medical access, community, family, ability, social environment, and physical location impact our journeys. I see this as get further in my own transition (while I've been doing this for quite some time now, there are many transition steps I've never been able to access) but have watched others come out years after me and make far quicker strides forward than me. I'm still wildly more privileged than many other folks, and I am so happy for any person who is able to do everything they need in their own transition, but I just believe it's pretty vital to remember that comparing and contrasting ourselves on a single axis is never sufficient.

written by dana rusty on 21/12/2023

I think so much discourse and personal conflict these days, especially online, can be seen more transparently when you recognize that many people are desperately grappling for coherent identity and community while being totally unaware what those things look like, and often are drawn into posturing and positioning themselves as trusted leaders and thinkers within those communities.

self-appointed false authorities will try and convince you that it's them versus the world, and that solidarity is transactional, all the while using the language of intersectionality and coalition. I do not believe these folks will not lead you to revolution, but they will profit off of half-baked ideas and divisiveness. it's possibly never been more important to ask yrself who you consider trustworthy and why.

written by dana rusty on 17/05/2022

trans people live in cis society and are told to play by cis rules. our expressions are not only misunderstood and ridiculed but routinely dampened and dimmed. our existence is seen as an attack because it challenges the systems which the cis population are told is innate and natural. anti-trans concepts are so deeply entrenched that many cis folks, even those that believe they support us, view our love and care for one another as exclusionary and oppressive of them. but how could I possibly deny something to those who are given everything?

I don't really desire trans politicians and trans leaders of government anymore; I increasingly desire trans society, because my vision of trans society is one where cis people could thrive, but I'm struggling to believe that trans people will never be able to fully exist in this cis society. the future that I want is not one where trans people are supreme in any way, but where trans people are considered in every aspect of life. where transition is seen for the magic it is, and not as a fix or a patch or a loss of innocence. this is our earth as well and we deserve to be seen as more than a denigration of the norm.
